Lasagna

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with layers of pasta, rich meat or vegetable sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, and melted cheese on top. Each layer is baked to perfection, creating a hearty and flavorful meal with a soft, yet slightly crispy texture. It is commonly prepared with ground beef or sausage in a tomato-based sauce, though vegetarian versions with spinach or mushrooms are also popular. The dish is known for its comforting, indulgent taste and is often enjoyed as a main course. Lasagna is versatile, with variations across different regions and families, but always remains a crowd favorite.

Ingredients are as follows:
  • Lasagna noodles (about 12–15, cooked)
  • Ground beef (or sausage, or a combination)
  • Marinara or tomato sauce (about 3–4 cups)
  • Ricotta cheese (or cottage cheese, about 15–16 oz)
  • Mozzarella cheese (shredded, about 2–3 cups)
Steps:
  1. Prepare the meat sauce: Sauté diced onion and garlic in olive oil, then add ground beef (or sausage) and cook until browned. Stir in marinara sauce, season with salt, pepper, and Italian seasoning, and let simmer for about 15 minutes.
  2. Cook the noodles: Boil lasagna noodles according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  3. Mix the cheese filling: In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese, eggs, Parmesan, and some seasoning. Stir until smooth.
  4. Layer the lasagna: In a baking dish, start with a layer of sauce, followed by noodles, then the ricotta mixture, and shredded mozzarella. Repeat the layers, ending with sauce and a final topping of mozzarella.
  5. Bake and serve: Cover the dish with foil and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25–30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for another 10–15 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden. Let it rest for 10 minutes before slicing.
